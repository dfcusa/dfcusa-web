TheDoGoodFromHomeChallenge

Use compassion and creativity to do acts of good from home
and fight racial injustice.

For every kind act shared with #DoGoodFromHome,
Hasbro will donate a toy or game to organizations providing care to the children of COVID-19 essential
workers, up to 25,000 toys and games.

present

Youth Leading for Equity

Given the current reality,
the #DoGoodFromHome challenges are focused on racial justice.

READ

Monday

June 8, 2020

Consider what you know about anti-racism.

Read an article or book about anti-racism. Here and here are lists.

Reflect on one lesson you learned. Discuss this lesson with a peer or adult.

LEARN

Tuesday

June 9, 2020

Research the words race, racism, anti-racism and ally (and co-conspirator for older students). Read about Bree Newsome and James Tyson and/or read about these teens that organized a 10,000 person Black Lives Matter Protest.

Reflect on one lesson you learned. Discuss this lesson with a peer or adult.

REFLECT

Wednesday

June 10, 2020

Choose to look at either the books you have or the movies or television shows you watch. Consider how many of the stories are written or told by Black people.

Reflect on what you discover.

Answer the question: What are you learning from Black authors and storytellers?

ACT

Thursday

June 11, 2020

List out ways that you see Black youth taking action. List out ways you see Black leaders taking action. List out ways you see anti-racist non-Black people taking action.

Reflect on what you can do. Take action.

Reflect on what you learn. Invite people to act with you.

RESPOND

Friday

June 12, 2020

Look at these common racist comments (from @wastefreemarie) and ways to respond.

Write down ways you can respond to racist comments.

Commit to responding to racist comments. Reflect on what you are learning.

Take a picture or video, share it on social media using

#DoGoodFromHome and challenge others!
